Discover Japan

Explore Japan’s most beautiful towns, castles, and coastlines, each with its own story and atmosphere.

Must-See Places Across Japan

Kanazawa – Garden & Samurai Districts

Home to Kenrokuen Garden, one of Japan’s most celebrated landscape gardens, and preserved samurai and teahouse districts.

  • Region: Ishikawa Prefectur
  • Best Time: Spring blossoms & autumn colors
  • Don’t miss: Kenrokuen, Higashi Chaya District

Nara – Temples & Friendly Deer

Japan’s first permanent capital, where giant Buddha statues, ancient temples, and free-roaming deer share the same park.

  • Region: Near Kyoto & Osaka
  • Best Time: Day trip in any season
  • Don’t Miss: Todai-ji, Nara Park, Kasuga Shrine

Nikkō – Forest Shrines & Waterfalls

Mountain town known for richly decorated shrines and temples surrounded by cedar forests, plus nearby lakes and waterfalls.

  • Region: North of Tokyo
  • Best Time: Autumn for vivid foliage
  • Don’t Miss: Tōshō-gū, Kegon Falls, Lake Chūzenji

Naoshima – Art Island

A small island filled with modern art museums, outdoor sculptures, and striking architecture by the sea.

  • Region: Seto Inland Sea
  • Best Time: Mild spring and autumn
  • Don’t Miss: Benesse House Museum, Yayoi Kusama pumpkins

Takayama – Old Town in the Mountains

A charming town in the Japanese Alps with wooden merchant houses, morning markets, and nearby traditional farm villages.

  • Region: Gifu Prefecture
  • Best Time: Spring & autumn festivals
  • Don’t Miss: Sanmachi Old Town, Hida Folk Village

Okinawa – Tropical Blue Seas

Island chain with clear water, coral reefs, and its own distinct culture, perfect for snorkeling and slow beach days.

  • Region: Far south of mainland Japan
  • Best Time: Late spring to early autumn
  • Don’t Miss: Snorkeling, local food, island hopping

Food & Seasonal Experiences

Each region in Japan has its own specialty dishes and seasonal events, from winter snow festivals to summer fireworks by the sea.

Spring – Sakura & Picnics

Join locals under the cherry blossoms for hanami picnics in parks, with bento boxes and seasonal sweets.

Summer – Festivals & Fireworks

Many towns host night festivals with food stalls, lanterns, and huge fireworks over rivers and bays.

Autumn – Colorful Mountains

Ride scenic trains or cable cars to see red and gold leaves covering valleys and temple grounds.

Winter – Snow & Hot Springs

Warm up in outdoor hot springs while snow falls around you, especially in the northern and mountain regions.
