Discover Taiwan

Explore Taiwans most beautiful towns, castles, and coastlines, each with its own story and atmosphere.

Must-See Places Across Taiwan

Taipei 101 – meter-tall skyscraper

Taipei 101 is a, 508-meter-tall skyscraper located in the Xinyi District of Taipei.It held the title of the world's tallest building from 2004 to 2010.

  • Region: Xinyi Dist
  • Best Time: ay trip in any season
  • Don’t miss: features a luxury shopping mall and an observatory offering a 360-degree bird's-eye view of the Taipei basin.

National Palace Museum – world's larges of Chinese

The National Palace Museum in Taipei houses the world's largest collection of Chinese artifacts.

  • Region: Shilin Dist
  • Best Time: Day trip in any season
  • Don’t Miss: Houses one of the world's largest collections of Chinese imperial artifacts, including the famous "Jadeite Cabbage" and "Meat-Shaped Stone."

Sun Moon Lake – The trail voted by CNN

It is best enjoyed by taking a boat cruise or cycling along the trail voted by CNN as one of the most beautiful in the world.

  • Region: Yuchi Township
  • Best Time: Autumn & Winter season
  • Don’t Miss: Taiwan’s largest freshwater lake. It is best enjoyed by taking a boat cruise or cycling along the trail voted by CNN as one of the most beautiful in the world.

Chihkan Tower – Tainan Confucius Temple

Built by the Dutch in 1653, Chihkan Tower is a premier historical site and iconic landmark.

  • Region: West Central Dist
  • Best Time: West Central District
  • Don’t Miss: ainan is the historical heart of Taiwan. This area is packed with ancient landmarks and legendary street food like Beef Soup and Eel Noodles.

Takayama – Old Town in the Mountains

Taroko National Park, located in eastern Taiwan, is renowned for its breathtaking marble canyons,high mountains, and the scenic Liwu River.

  • Region: Xiulin Township
  • Best Time: West Central District
  • Don’t Miss: World-famous for its marble canyons. Key sights include the Swallow Grotto (Yanzikou), Tunnel of Nine Turns, and Eternal Spring Shrine.

Qixingtan Beach – Tropical Blue Seas

Qixingtan Beach, located in Hualien, is a stunning crescent-shaped bay known for its crystal-clear blue waters, scenic pebble beaches, and dramatic mountain views.

  • Region: Township
  • Best Time: Late spring to early autumn
  • Don’t Miss: A stunning crescent-shaped bay covered in smooth cobble stones, offering views of the Pacific Ocean and the distant Qingshui Cliff.

Seasonal Event

Join Taiwan local seasonal event with your Trip

Hakka Tung Blossom Festival (April – May)

Known as "May Snow," white petals cover the mountain trails of Taoyuan, Hsinchu, and Miaoli.

Nuit Blanche Taipei (October)

An all-night arts festival where the city stays awake for light installations, performances, and interactive art scattered across urban spaces.

Silver Grass Season at Caoling Ancient Trail (November)

Hikers flock to the northeast coast to see the mountainsides covered in swaying, silver-white miscanthus flowers.

Shirakawa-go Winter Illumination (Jan–Feb)

The UNESCO-listed farmhouse village looks like a fairy tale under the snow and lights.
